Frequently asked Questions

What destinations do you offer trekking packages for?

We offer trekking packages to various destinations including Everest Base Camp, Annapurna Circuit, Langtang Valley, Mustang, and many more scenic trails across Nepal.

What is the best time of year to trek in Nepal?

The best time to trek in Nepal is during the spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) seasons when the weather is generally clear and stable, offering the best views and comfortable trekking conditions.

Do I need to be physically fit to go on a trek?

While some treks are more challenging than others, it's advisable to have a basic level of fitness to enjoy trekking in Nepal. Regular exercise and moderate fitness levels will enhance your trekking experience.

What is the duration of a typical trekking trip?

The duration of trekking trips varies depending on the route and itinerary. Generally, treks range from 7 to 21 days, including both trekking days and rest/acclimatization days.

What kind of accommodations can I expect during the trek?

Accommodations during the trek vary from basic tea houses to comfortable lodges with amenities like hot showers and Wi-Fi in popular trekking regions. In remote areas, accommodations are more basic with shared facilities.

Do I need to hire a guide and porter?

While hiring a guide and porter is not mandatory, it's highly recommended for safety, navigation, and cultural insights. Guides and porters enhance your trekking experience by providing valuable assistance and support throughout the journey.

Is altitude sickness a concern during trekking?

Altitude sickness can affect trekkers at high elevations. Our itineraries are designed to allow for proper acclimatization, and our guides are trained to recognize symptoms and take appropriate measures to ensure your safety.

What should I pack for a trekking trip?

Essential items include sturdy hiking boots, warm clothing layers, a sleeping bag, sunscreen, a hat, sunglasses, a first-aid kit, and personal toiletries. We provide a detailed packing list for your convenience.

Are permits required for trekking in Nepal?

Yes, trekking permits are required for most trekking regions in Nepal. Our team assists you in obtaining the necessary permits and ensures compliance with local regulations.

What safety measures are in place during the trek?

Our experienced guides prioritize safety at all times. They conduct thorough safety briefings, monitor weather conditions, carry communication devices, and are trained in first aid and emergency response procedures.
